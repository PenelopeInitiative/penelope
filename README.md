# Penelope

**Making scams unprofitable, one wasted hour at a time.**

Scamming is a numbers game. Sending a million fraudulent emails costs nothing; robocalls cost fractions of a cent. But closing a victim — the long conversation, the built trust, the extraction — costs the one thing scam operations cannot scale for free: **time**. Human closer hours, clean phone numbers, aged accounts, mule bandwidth. Every scam that succeeds pays for thousands that don't.

For decades, defense meant awareness campaigns. Global scam losses passed $1 trillion a year anyway.

Conversational AI changes the math. For the first time, defense can field unlimited, patient, convincing conversational labor at a marginal cost approaching the scammer's send cost. An AI persona that keeps a scam operator talking for forty minutes hasn't just protected one inbox — it has burned closer time, exposed phone numbers to flagging, and extended mule-account exposure. Done at scale, it taxes the entire operation until the business proposition collapses.

Named for Penelope of Ithaca, who held off her suitors for years by weaving a shroud by day and unweaving it by night. She never attacked anyone. She just made them waste their time.

## The gap we fill

Every component is already proven: time-wasting phone bots (Lenny, Jolly Roger, O2's *Daisy*), email engagement bots (Netsafe's *Re:scam*), telco-level AI diversion (Apate.ai), human scambaiting at scale (Kitboga, Scammer Payback). What doesn't exist is what made web security scale — **a vendor-neutral standard and a consortium behind it**, on the OWASP model:

- **The Engagement Standard** — how scam traffic is redirected to AI personas: routing protocols, persona requirements, inference caps, data handling
- **A donated-inference pool** — AI providers contribute bounded capacity; contributions are audited and publicly attributed
- **The Scam Economics Index** — published quarterly: scammer-hours burned, campaigns disrupted, indicators shared with law enforcement

## Founding principles

1. **Inbound only.** Personas never initiate contact. They engage traffic sent to opted-in users or verified honeypots. There is no "submit any number" feature, and there never will be.
2. **Target operations, not individuals.** Many front-line operators in scam compounds are trafficking victims. No harassment theater, no humiliation content — we degrade economics, coordinated with anti-trafficking bodies.
3. **Open methods, audited data.** Everything is public except persona playbooks (the one thing fingerprinting attackers must not have). Data is minimized, independently audited, never repurposed.
4. **Vendor-neutral by bylaw.** No single provider controls routing, governance, or the standard.
